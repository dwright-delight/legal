Privacy Policy

Last updated: 8 May 2026

1. About this policy

This Privacy Policy describes how Danny Wright, trading as Decide Wright (ABN 86 320 187 573) ("Decide Wright", "we", "us", "our"), handles personal information in connection with the software services we provide, including the TuffRock Factory Manager (the "Factory Manager") — a private application built and operated for TuffRock Pty Ltd ("TuffRock") to manage their inventory and manufacturing operations.

Decide Wright is the operator of the Factory Manager. TuffRock is the customer for whom the Factory Manager is operated, and is the controller of the QuickBooks Online and manufacturing data the Factory Manager processes.

2. Whose data we handle

  • TuffRock staff who use the Factory Manager (operators, office staff, administrators).
  • Records held in TuffRock's QuickBooks Online (QBO) company file, which the Factory Manager accesses via Intuit's official APIs under TuffRock's authorisation. These records may contain personal information about TuffRock's customers and suppliers (e.g. names, contact details, billing addresses).

The Factory Manager is not offered to the public and does not collect personal information from members of the public.

3. What we process and why

3.1 TuffRock staff authentication and audit

  • Username, email, role, and an encrypted hash of the user's password — to authenticate staff into the Factory Manager.
  • Session identifiers — to maintain logged-in state.
  • Audit log entries that record which authorised user performed which action and when — for traceability and accountability within TuffRock's operations.

3.2 QuickBooks Online records

The Factory Manager reads and writes the following QBO entities under TuffRock's authorisation: customers, items (products), inventory levels, sales orders (Estimates), vendors, purchase orders, item receipts, bills, and invoices. We do not extract this data for any purpose unrelated to operating the Factory Manager.

3.3 Manufacturing operational data

Work orders, production sessions and runs, batch and lot records, quality checks, dispatch records, and supporting documentation captured by TuffRock staff during normal operations.

4. Sub-processors

We use the following service providers to operate the Factory Manager. They process data on our instructions and are subject to confidentiality and security obligations:

  • Railway (Railway Corp, USA) — application hosting and managed PostgreSQL database hosting.
  • Sentry (Functional Software Inc., USA) — application error monitoring. Diagnostic information about runtime errors (stack traces, request metadata) may be transmitted to Sentry. We configure Sentry to avoid forwarding personal information beyond what is technically required to identify and correct faults.
  • Intuit (Intuit Inc., USA) — QuickBooks Online is the source-of-truth system for the entities listed in section 3.2. Data is exchanged with Intuit only on TuffRock's authorisation, in accordance with Intuit's terms.
  • GitHub (GitHub Inc., USA) — source-code hosting. Production data is not stored in GitHub.

Some of these providers are located outside Australia. By authorising the Factory Manager to connect to TuffRock's QBO company, TuffRock consents to this cross-border processing for the purpose of operating the service.

5. Storage and security

We take reasonable steps to protect personal information from misuse, loss, and unauthorised access, including:

  • TLS in transit for all connections to the Factory Manager.
  • Encrypted storage of sensitive credentials (QBO refresh tokens are sealed using libsodium curve25519 keys before being persisted).
  • Password hashing with Argon2id for staff accounts.
  • Role-based access controls within the Factory Manager.
  • Append-only audit logging of mutating operations.

No system can be guaranteed secure, and we do not represent that the service is invulnerable to all attacks.

6. Retention and deletion

We retain Factory Manager data for as long as TuffRock's engagement with us is active and for a reasonable period afterwards to satisfy legal, tax, and operational record-keeping obligations. On termination, we will return or delete TuffRock's data in accordance with the engagement agreement and any applicable retention requirements.

7. Your rights

If you are an Australian resident and we hold personal information about you, the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles give you the right to access and correct that information, and to make a complaint about our handling of it. Contact us using the details in section 9. We will respond within a reasonable period.

If you are a TuffRock employee, you may also raise privacy questions internally with TuffRock, who is the controller of the data the Factory Manager processes.

We may update this policy as our service or legal obligations change. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page indicates when the most recent change was made.
